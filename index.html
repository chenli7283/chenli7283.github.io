About me

I received the M.S. and PhD degrees in Engineering from Hiroshima University, Japan, in 2016 and 2019, respectively.
In 2019 and 2020, I was a visiting researcher with the Graduate School of Advanced Science and Engineering, Hiroshima University, Japan.
Since 2021, I has been a research fellow with the Department of Bioscience and Bioinformatics, Faculty of Computer Science and Systems Engineering, Kyushu Institute of Technology, Japan.
I am currently a JSPS research fellow with the Department of Complex Systems Science, Graduate School of Informatics, Nagoya University, Japan.
I focus on using AI to solve practical problems.

Research

My research interests include:

  • Deep Learning Approaches: Variational autoencoder (VAE), Generative adversarial network (GAN), Flow-based generative models, Diffusion models

  • Reinforcement Learning Applications: Q-learning, Deep Q-network (DQN), Policy gradient, Actor-critic.

  • Distributed Data Mining: Area skyline query, MapReduce algorithms, Apache Spark framework.

  • Performance Evaluation: Queueing theory, Stochastic processess, Markov chain.

Current work

  • TransORGAN: Transformer-based Objective-reinforced GAN for De Novo Molecular Generation (IJCAI-ECAI 2021)

  • TenGAN: Pure Transformer Encoder-based GAN for Molecular design with Property Optimization

  • SpotGAN: Reverse-transformer GAN generates Scaffold-constrained Molecules with Property Optimization (ECML-PKDD 2023)

  • Gx2Mol: De Novo Drug Generation of Therapeutics from Gene Expression Profile Data

  • GxVAEs: Two VAEs can Generate Hit-like Molecules from Gene Expression Profiles
